What is Reiki?

Since its beginning in Japan, Reiki has been adapted across varying cultural traditions. It uses a technique commonly called palm healing or hands-on-healing. Through the use of this technique, practitioners believe that they are transferring "universal energy" through the palms of the practitioner, which they believe encourages healing.

A Reiki session usually is between 60 and 90 minutes in length. The session is performed by using specific Reiki hand positions by a Reiki practitioner or master. During a session, the client is fully clothed while comfortably reclining on a massage table or seated in a chair. A Reiki session may help ease tension and stress and may help support the body to facilitate an environment for healing on all levels- physical, emotional and spiritual. A session is pleasant and relaxing and is most often utilized for one’s own personal wellness.

About Christina

About Christina

My passion for Reiki began as a rediscovery of my love for bodywork, after the birth of my daughter. The calming effects of Reiki on my own body and mind helped me access a deep and heightened state of meditation. I decided to expand on my Reiki education at Maha Rose in Brooklyn, under the guidance of Lisa Levin and Erika Spring. Through Reiki, I discovered an inner peace I had not previously experienced, and am now eager to bring this into the lives of my clientele.

In my previous life, I studied modalities in Ayurveda at the Aveda Institute and became an aesthetician. I eventually transitioned into massage therapy at the Southwest Institute of Healing Arts, where I gained insight into so many wonderful avenues of the healing world.

My skill sets of Ayurvedic balance, massage, and now Reiki have allowed me to truly provide the most in-depth and well-rounded bodywork to my clients. I constantly strive to expand my knowledge in the pursuit of providing clients with the best possible care and help my client reach a state of enlightenment.
